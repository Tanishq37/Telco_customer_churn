# Customer Churn Analysis using SQL

## Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing customer churn behavior for a telecom company using SQL. The goal was to identify why customers leave the company and uncover patterns that can help improve customer retention.

The dataset contains **7,043 customer records** with information such as demographics, contract type, payment method, internet service, tenure, monthly charges, and churn status.

## Objective
- Analyze churn percentage across customer segments
- Identify high-risk customers likely to leave
- Understand factors affecting churn
- Provide business recommendations to improve retention

## Tools & Skills Used
- SQL (MySQL)
- Data Cleaning
- Aggregations
- CASE WHEN
- GROUP BY
- Percentage Analysis
- Business Insights

## Key Analysis Performed
### 1. Overall Customer Churn
- Total Customers: **7,043**
- Churned Customers: **1,869**
- Overall Churn Rate: **26.54%**

### 2. Churn by Contract Type
- Month-to-Month customers had the highest churn (**42.71%**)
- One-Year contracts had lower churn (**11.27%**)
- Two-Year contracts had the lowest churn (**2.83%**)

### 3. Churn by Payment Method
- Electronic Check users had highest churn (**45%**)
- Automatic payment users had much lower churn

### 4. Churn by Age Group
- Senior Citizens churned more (**41.68%**) than younger customers

### 5. Churn by Internet Service
- Fiber Optic users had highest churn (**41.89%**)

### 6. Churn by Tenure
- Customers in first year had highest churn (**47.44%**)

### 7. Monthly Charges Impact
- Churned customers had higher average monthly charges (**74.44**) than retained customers (**61.27**)

## Business Insights
- Long-term contracts improve retention significantly
- Automatic payment methods reduce churn
- Senior citizens require targeted retention plans
- Fiber optic pricing/service quality may need review
- First-year customer experience is critical
- High monthly charges increase churn risk

## Recommendations
1. Encourage monthly users to shift to yearly plans
2. Promote auto-pay discounts
3. Improve onboarding for new customers
4. Review fiber optic service complaints and pricing
5. Offer loyalty discounts to high-bill customers
6. Launch special retention campaigns for senior citizens
